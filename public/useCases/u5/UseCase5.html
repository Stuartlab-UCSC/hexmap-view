Objective: Identify squamous-like bladder cancer samples from the TCGA PanCan-12 mRNA dataset.

Skills Covered:

  • Filtering the samples in the map using a short list selection.
  • Creating a group using polygon selection.
  • Sorting sample attributes based on their association with a custom group.

Steps:

  1. In the Tumor Map, make sure the mRNA layout is selected

  1. In the Short List, make sure Tissue is checked and no other attributes are checked
  2. Under the Tissue attribute click on the filter checkbox and select BLCA in the drop down

  1. Observe that only BLCA samples are now colored in the map

  1. Locate the zoom in/out control in the bottom of the right of the map screen and click on the + several times to zoom in to see BLCA tumors closer

  1. Click on Select By drop down and select Polygon
  2. Select squamous BLCA samples (those in the LUSC and HNSC region)

  1. Specify the name of the group (e.g. SquamousBLCA) in the popup box
  2. Click on the Sort Attributes button (next to Sorted By label)
  3. In the popup click on Focus attribute radio button
  4. Select SquamousBLCA as the focus attribute
  5. Click Sort

  1. Wait until Computing statistics now … disappears
  2. Observe that the top differentiating attribute in the Select Attribute drop down list is squamous gene program (see the illustration)
